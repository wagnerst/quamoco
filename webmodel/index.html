<h3>Structure of the Quality Model</h3>
Here we give a brief overview of the structure of the quality model.
<img src="ModelStructure.png" alt="Structure of the Quamoco Quality Model" width="650"/>
<p>
The main concept of the quality model are <i>Factors</i>. A factor expresses a property of an
entity. We describe with entities the things that are important for quality. Properties describe
the attributes of the things we are interested in.
</p>
<p>This concept of a factor is rather general. Thus, we use it on two levels of abstraction:
<ol>
<li><i>Quality Aspects</i> describe abstract quality goals defined for the whole product. In
our quality model we use the "-ilities" of ISO 25010 as quality aspects. Typical examples
for such quality aspects are <i>Maintainability</i>, <i>Analysability</i>, and <i>Modifiability</i>.
</li>
<li><i>Product Factors</i> describe concrete, measurable properties of concrete entities. An example
for a factor is the <i>Complexity of a method</i>, which can be measured by the cyclomatic complexity
number, or by the nesting depth of the method.
</li>
</ol>
</p>
<p>
To close the gap between abstract quality aspects and measurable product factors, we need to set
the product factors in relation to the quality aspects. This is done via <i>Impacts</i>. An impact
is either positive or negative and describes how the degree of presence or absence of a product factor
influences a quality aspect.
</p>
<p>
A third layer in the levels of abstraction are <i>Measures</i>, which describe how a specify product factor
can be quantified. To realize the connection to concrete tools in a quality assessment, we further introduce
<i>Instruments</i>. An instrument describes a concrete implementation of a measure. For the example of the
nesting depth, an instrument is the corresponding metric as implemented in the quality analysis framework
ConQAT. This way, different tools can be used for a single measure.
</p>
<p>
In order to fully operationalize the quality model we need to specify aggregation formulas. They are
called <i>Evaluations</i> and they are assigned to the factors in the quality model.
</p>
